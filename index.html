What are some Historical Figures in the world of IT?

Radia Perlman

The Bio: Radia Perlman is a software designer and network engineer who literally wrote the rules for how computers talk to each other. In the 1980s, networks used to crash constantly if there were too many connections (data would just loop endlessly). The Ultimate Achievement: She invented the Spanning Tree Protocol (STP). This algorithm allowed network bridges and switches to figure out the best path for data to travel without creating massive, server-crashing loops. She essentially made the massive, modern structure of the Internet possible!

Grace Hopper

Rear Admiral Grace Hopper was a computer scientist and military legend. Back in the day, code was just 1s and 0s, which took forever to write. She believed code should be written in a language humans could actually read. The Ultimate Achievement: She invented the first Compiler—a program that translates English-like instructions into machine code. This paved the way for modern programming languages like Java and C#. (She also popularized the term "debugging" after pulling an actual, physical moth out of a broken computer relay!)

Dorothy Vaughan

Dorothy Vaughan was a brilliant mathematician and the first Black supervisor at the National Advisory Committee for Aeronautics (which later became NASA). When electronic computers first arrived, she realized that the human mathematicians (called "computers" at the time) were going to lose their jobs to machines. The Ultimate Achievement: Instead of giving up, she taught herself FORTRAN, one of the very first high-level programming languages. Then, she taught it to her entire staff of Black women. She transformed her team from human calculators into some of the first professional software engineers in aerospace history.
