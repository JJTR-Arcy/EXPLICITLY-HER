Mary Joy Olivarez | BSIT 1-2

People can sometimes be mistaken about the concept of the IT program and think that only male students are capable of creating a career in the IT field or just that many students are only male in this program. However, in my experience it’s mostly equal. Because there are plenty of female students who I’ve seen in the field of IT, which could also mean that they are capable of working alongside any male’s job and have the power to lead and get the job done. With women being responsible, persistent, and goal-aligned, nothing could limit us, only one’s mind when perception is gained. I would even say, try to do hard things, and be comfortable with being embarrassed because that’s when you know you’re trying.

I admit that college programs should not be taken lightly and have their own educational levels. However, when it comes to the IT field, back in my first semester it was hard for me to adjust to the new people around me, the new environment, and the new program I needed to fully embrace. And even though this program is not my first option, I came to figure it out that I’m here to take my shot and be successful. Knowing that programming is hard for me to take up and that I’m from the STEM strand in senior high school, I still know that I can do it and learn from the beginning as an IT student. With people around me that I can share my knowledge with, the friends, acquaintances, and professors that I can count on, I know I won’t be alone in this fight because we can grow, be challenged, and learn from each other.

As someone who is in the first year of college, I am the person who’s still becoming to be one i aspire in the future. With the small wins in the last semester, i have been acknowledge as someone that can lead through projects and group presentations. With people around me, they believe that i can do hard things and can achieve it if i try. Which i do believe too, that’s why lately I signed up for the junior leadership in EXPLICIT, trying to get out from my comfort zone and start by creating small steps that can lead me to my success.

What are the historical women figures in the field of I.T

Ada Lovelace

Considered the first computer programmer. She worked on Charles Babbage's early mechanical general-purpose computer, the Analytical Engine.

Grace Hopper

A computer scientist and U.S. Navy rear admiral who helped develop the first compiler for a computer programming language.

Katherine Johnson

A mathematician whose calculations of orbital mechanics were critical to the success of the first and subsequent U.S. manned spaceflights.
